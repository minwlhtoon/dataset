Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that enables machines to simulate human intelligence, including learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making. It uses algorithms and vast amounts of data to identify patterns, understand language, and perform tasks that typically require human cognition, such as image recognition or driving cars.

Key Aspects of AI
Definition: AI involves developing computer systems capable of performing complex tasks that once required human intelligence.
How it Works: AI relies on technologies like machine learning (ML), deep learning, and neural networks, rather than just rigid, programmed instructions. It learns by analyzing data, similar to how a person learns from experience.
Types of Capabilities:
Perception: Recognizing speech and images.
Cognition: Reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making.
Generative: Creating new content, such as text, images, and music (e.g., ChatGPT).
Common Applications: AI powers virtual assistants (Siri, Alexa), recommendation engines (Netflix, Amazon), autonomous vehicles, and customer service chatbots.
Goal: The ultimate goal is to create systems that can operate autonomously to achieve defined goals, such as automating repetitive tasks or detecting fraudulent transactions.

Key Subfields
Machine Learning (ML): Teaching computers to learn from data to improve performance without explicit programming.
Deep Learning: A subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with many layers, often used for complex tasks like facial recognition.
Natural Language Processing (NLP): Enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
