Cats are popular, low-maintenance pets suitable for various living situations, including apartments, often living 15-20 years. They are independent yet affectionate, self-grooming, and easy to litter-train. Costs include food, litter, and veterinary care, requiring a long-term commitment to their health and well-being. 
Key Aspects of Cat Ownership
Pros: They are relatively quiet, do not need to be walked, and offer companionship. They are excellent for urban living and can help keep homes free of pests.
Costs: While generally cheaper than dogs, expenses include initial adoption fees,, food, litter, and routine or emergency vet care.
Behavior: Cats are independent but form strong bonds with owners, sometimes acting aloof. They require mental stimulation through play and need scratching, hiding, and climbing spots.
Care Requirements: A primary caretaker should be designated to ensure they receive daily love, affection, and proper care.
Considerations: Before adopting, it is crucial to ensure everyone in the household is on board and to check if your living situation (e.g., renting) allows pets. 
Tips for New Cat Owners
The "Rule of Three": Cats often need time to adjust, generally following a pattern of 3 days to feel safe, 3 weeks to settle into a routine, and 3 months to feel at home.
Safety: Ensure your home is safe, and provide toys to encourage natural behaviors like chasing and hunting.
Health: Regular veterinary check-ups are necessary to keep them healthy and prevent the spread of diseases.
Environment: They enjoy vertical space, so cat trees or shelves are recommended for apartments.
